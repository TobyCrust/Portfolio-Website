Vert Vault A portable case for skating items that doubles as a filming stand

Traveling to a skate park can be a hassle when you have numerous loose items to manage. Bulky bags are inconvenient, and relying on pockets often leads to lost or damaged belongings. The Vert Vault - a durable and impact-resistant case is designed to provide a secure and organized solution. With its unique features, including an intergrated bipod, foam inserts for phones, adjustable straps, and unique filming capabilities,

This project helped me to understand how to cut down and restrain my design. To create a device that fills its purpose with precision. I wanted to create a product that's function was inherent in its design, one you could tell exactly what you were buying from a glance.

B-F Skate Pants A balance visualiser for skating and snowbaording

The B-F (Balance-force) Skate pants are a unique fusion of fashion, technology, and skateboarding. Created collaboration, these LED jeans offer an additional form of self-expression for the skateboarding community. Pressure sensors are embedded within the shoes which respond to the subtle shifts in a skateboarder’s weight distribution. When the skater leans forward, a cascade of lights runs up the front of the leg, while a lean backward illuminates a path up the back of the leg. Additionally, when a turn is sustained for more than 3 seconds, the light colour changes to let the skater know they have completed a technical manoeuvre. These visual displays are made possible using Arduino technology and Neo-pixels discreetly integrated into the garment. These LEDs are diffused by 3D printed gems which are placed above the LED light strip. This project serves a dual purpose: it's not only a fashion statement but also a dynamic tool for skateboarders to visualize their force and balance in real-time. Whether at the skate park or cruising through the streets, this wearable piece creates a visual spectacle that mirrors the skater's skill and finesse.

This project was chosen to be featured in the 2023 end of year Victoria University of Wellington design exhabition.

Photon Blade A light up 3D printed sword that changes colour with movement and pressure.

The Photon Blade is a 3D-printed movement and pressure-activated sword. Embedded within the hilt is a pressure sensor that seamlessly translates pressure into the luminosity of the blade. An accelerometer reads the movement and direction of the sword and changes colour accordingly. The blade is made of a transparent filament whereas the base is made of UV changing filament wrapped in durable paracord.

The Thermopus The "ThermoPus" is a health and safety device used to alert the user to the risks of hypothermia. It stops surfers in cold water conditions from staying in for too long.

Throughout this project, I acquired diverse workflow skills. While the overall concept for the final product remained relatively consistent from the outset, the primary challenge revolved around addressing the engineering hurdles associated with developing a compact, cost-effective, and waterproof device designed to be worn on the body. The design and material selection underwent numerous iterations, incorporating initial PLA scale models, flexible TPU prototypes, various molds, diverse circuit designs, and test models produced on a Stratasys J750 printer. This process allowed for a comprehensive exploration of design possibilities and a meticulous consideration of the engineering intricacies involved in creating an innovative wearable product.

This project was chosen to be showcased in the 2023 end of year design exhabition
